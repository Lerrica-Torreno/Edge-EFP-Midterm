Make it Better

✅ Keep your CV clear and concise (1–2 pages max).
✅ Focus on achievements, not just job responsibilities.
✅ Tailor your CV for each role you’re applying for.
✅ Use action verbs like developed, implemented, improved.
✅ Highlight measurable results (e.g., “Improved system efficiency by 20%”).
✅ Keep formatting clean and consistent (fonts, spacing, bullet points).
✅ Don’t forget to proofread – avoid spelling/grammar mistakes.
✅ Include only relevant information (cut unnecessary details).

Be prepared

Q1: Tell me about yourself.

A: I’m a recent Computer Science graduate with strong web dev and data analysis skills. During my internship, I improved the reporting system, reducing manual work by 30%. I’m passionate about solving problems with technology.
Q2: Why should we hire you?

A: I have the technical skills in Python, JavaScript, and C++ plus the drive to contribute. I adapt quickly, collaborate well in teams, and I’m eager to learn while delivering results.
Q3: What are your strengths and weaknesses?

A: Strengths – detail-oriented, adaptable, fast learner. Weakness – I used to over-focus on details but now balance quality with efficiency.
Q4: Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

A: I see myself as a skilled IT professional leading impactful projects, with certifications and leadership skills, contributing to company goals.
