Tips & Tricks

✅ Research the company before your interview.
✅ Practice common interview questions out loud.
✅ Highlight real experiences when answering.
✅ Be honest about your weaknesses but show improvement.
✅ Emphasize teamwork and communication skills.
✅ Ask thoughtful questions at the end of the interview.
✅ Follow up with a thank-you email.
✅ Stay confident but humble.

Be prepared

Q1: Can you describe a project where you applied your technical skills?

Applicant: During my final year, I developed a web-based inventory system using Python (Flask), HTML, and JavaScript. The system automated stock updates and reduced manual errors by 40%. I worked with a small team, managed version control using GitHub, and presented the system to stakeholders. This project improved my teamwork, problem-solving, and full-stack development skills.
Q2: How do you keep your technical skills up to date?

Applicant: I regularly take online courses on platforms like Coursera and freeCodeCamp. I also participate in coding challenges on HackerRank and contribute to small open-source projects on GitHub. This helps me stay updated with the latest tools, frameworks, and best practices.
Q3: How do you handle tight deadlines or pressure in IT projects?

Applicant: I break the project into smaller tasks and prioritize deliverables. I use tools like Trello or Jira to stay organized and communicate early with my team about blockers. In my internship, I delivered a reporting feature within a week by focusing on core functionality first, which ensured we met the deadline.
Q4: Why do you want to work in IT with our company?

Applicant: I admire your company’s work in cloud computing and innovative software solutions. With my skills in Python, C++, and JavaScript, I believe I can contribute to scalable solutions. At the same time, I see this role as an opportunity to grow my expertise while contributing to impactful projects.
