Make it better

✅ Keep your CV clear and concise (1–2 pages max).
✅ Focus on achievements, not just job responsibilities.
✅ Tailor your CV for each role you’re applying for.
✅ Use action verbs like developed, implemented, improved.
✅ Highlight measurable results (e.g., “Improved system efficiency by 20%”).
✅ Keep formatting clean and consistent (fonts, spacing, bullet points).
✅ Don’t forget to proofread – avoid spelling/grammar mistakes.
✅ Include only relevant information (cut unnecessary details).

Be prepared

Q1: Can you describe a project where you applied your technical skills?
A: During my final year, I developed a web-based inventory system using Python (Flask), HTML, and JavaScript. The system automated stock updates and reduced manual errors by 40%. I worked with a small team, managed version control using GitHub, and presented the system to stakeholders. This project improved my teamwork, problem-solving, and full-stack development skills.
Q2: How do you keep your technical skills up to date?
A: I regularly take online courses on platforms like Coursera and freeCodeCamp. I also participate in coding challenges on HackerRank and contribute to small open-source projects on GitHub. This helps me stay updated with the latest tools, frameworks, and best practices.
Q3: How do you handle tight deadlines or pressure in IT projects?
A: I break the project into smaller tasks and prioritize deliverables. I use tools like Trello or Jira to stay organized and communicate early with my team about blockers. In my internship, I delivered a reporting feature within a week by focusing on core functionality first, which ensured we met the deadline.
Q4: Why do you want to work in IT with our company?
A: I admire your company’s work in cloud computing and innovative software solutions. With my skills in Python, C++, and JavaScript, I believe I can contribute to scalable solutions. At the same time, I see this role as an opportunity to grow my expertise while contributing to impactful projects.
